📐 How to Measure Your Foot

  • Place your foot on a piece of paper
  • Mark the heel and longest toe
  • Measure the distance in cm
  • Compare with the chart above
  • Measure both feet — use the larger size

⚠️ Important Notes

  • Sneakers often run half-size large
  • Measure feet at the end of the day
  • Wear the socks you'll use with the shoes
  • Width matters — wide feet may need +0.5

How to Measure Yourself

Follow these simple steps to get accurate measurements every time

📌 Before You Start: Wear fitted clothing (not bulky). Use a soft measuring tape. Stand naturally — don't pull the tape too tight. Have a friend help if possible for best accuracy.

1

Chest / Bust

Wrap the tape around the fullest part of your chest, under your arms. Keep the tape parallel to the floor. Breathe normally — don't puff out your chest.

2

Waist

Find your natural waistline (above your belly button, below your ribs). Wrap the tape around comfortably. You should be able to fit one finger between the tape and your body.

3

Hips

Stand with feet together. Measure around the fullest part of your hips and buttocks. Keep the tape level all the way around.

4

Inseam (for pants)

Measure from the crotch seam down the inside of your leg to where you want the pants to end. For full length, measure to the ankle bone.

5

Foot Length

Stand on a piece of paper. Mark your heel and longest toe. Measure the distance in centimeters. Measure both feet and use the larger measurement.

6

Wrist (for watches)

Wrap the tape just below your wrist bone (where you'd wear a watch). Add 1cm for comfort. This is your wrist circumference.

✅ Best Practices

  • Measure in the morning for most accurate results
  • Use a soft, flexible measuring tape
  • Don't hold your breath while measuring
  • Take each measurement twice to confirm
  • Write down measurements in cm AND inches

❌ Common Mistakes

  • Pulling the tape too tight
  • Measuring over bulky clothing
  • Not keeping tape parallel to floor
  • Measuring only one foot/hand
  • Guessing your size instead of measuring
